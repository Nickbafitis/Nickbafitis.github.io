Plane Widefield Tutorial

Planning

The planning here requires the most attention as you can't just bring anything on a plane, you can only bring what fits in your carry on.
I recommend you make sure you have a window seat and bring the items listed below:

  • A camera
  • A small tripod
  • a lens cover(you can buy one online or use a cover/headrest on the plane)
  • extra battery, power supply, and memory
  • shuter release cable/built in timer
  • some stabilizer (for example a cushion)

Imaging

This part isn't as useful as it depends on your environment,
for example my tripod was small enough to fit in between the seats and that's how I took all of my pictures,
but I understand this option might not be available to everybody, so I recommend trying your best to find a secure position for your tripod.
In terms of exposure I recommend taking about 5-15 seconds depending on the flight and experimenting with the ISO, also remember to use the
shutter release cable or add an interval between the exposure and when you click the shutter button using the camera settings.

Processing

For processing I recommend Photoshop, but if your are unwilling to spend money on your processing software, there are several alternatives


I recommend following this workflow(feel free to follow your own though):
  1. First adjust the levels by clicking control/command+l
    and adjust the black slider until it is at the beginning of the histogram
    and slide the grey slider slightly to the left, repeat until you like the results, but be careful about how much noise you introduce.
  2. Adjust the saturation.
  3. Open camera raw on Photoshop and take away reflections (if there are any) and play around with the features.
  4. Enjoy results!(consider printing/publishing the image)
